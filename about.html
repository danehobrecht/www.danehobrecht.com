About

Sometimes, when I'm acting in vain, I get the idea that it’s me doing it. The reality is that, when I'm truly performing, if you smile, laugh, cry, or love, you’re doing so with God. My job is just to show you the door. I've recieved a lot of criticism for that belief, with most comments boiling down to that supposed fact that I'm needlessly rejecting the credit to my own work.

The idea that I'm moving something along that's a lot bigger than myself carries a universe more of meaning to me than taking ownership of something I don't think is mine to begin with. If I go back and recall what I find were my most "divine" moments on stage, all of them were when I, myself, wasn't doing anything. They were when I let go. Becoming the wire, rather than thinking I was the power.

This kind of work calls for an intense amount of vulnerability. It's something I feel as if I am naturally more inclined to be comfortable with. Call it a gift. Storytelling is something that's helped me (and what I hope to be others as well) through a lot of hardship. If you'd like me to tell yours, my contact information is in the resume tab.

The photograph to the left is one of my poppy, one of the greatest storytellers to ever do it. Without him, I wouldn't have been close to the person I am today. That's why I do what I do.
