Storytelling

Sometimes, when I'm acting in vain, I get the idea that it’s me doing it. The reality is that, if I'm truly performing, when people smile, laugh, cry, or love, they're doing so seperately with God. My job is just to show them the door. I've recieved a lot comments regarding that belief, with most boiling down to the supposed fact that I'm needlessly rejecting the credit to my own work.

The idea that I'm moving something along a lot bigger than myself carries a universe more of meaning to me than taking ownership of something I don't even think is mine to begin with. If I go back and recall what I find were my most important moments on stage, all of them were when I, myself, wasn't doing anything. They were when I let go and became the wire, rather than thinking I was the power.

Storytelling is something that's helped me (and what I hope to be others as well) through hardship and uncertainty. It's the kind of work that calls for an intense amount of vulnerability, and that's something I feel as if I am naturally more inclined to be comfortable with. God has embedded a story in each of our hearts, and it's important to me that I'm telling it right. The photograph displayed on this page is probably one of the reasons why. It features my Poppy, one of the funniest storytellers to ever do it. He married one of the biggest hearts the world has ever seen. Without them, I wouldn't have been close to the person I am today. That's why I do what I do.

If you'd like me to tell your story, my contact information is in the "Resume" tab.
